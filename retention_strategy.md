# Strategic Retainer Playbook & Capital Allocation Matrix

## 1. Empirically Quantified Customer Segments
Based on our data processing, our cohort population is divided into five distinct behavioral blocks:
* **Regular Value Core (1,118 Customers)**: Consistent baseline purchasers forming the stable foundation of our brand revenue[cite: 3].
* **New Shoppers (515 Customers)**: First-time buyers acquired within the last 60 days who require onboarding support[cite: 3].
* **At-Risk High-Value (428 Customers)**: High-revenue accounts who have crossed the 90-day inactivity threshold[cite: 3].
* **Champions (292 Customers)**: Highly active advocates with high transaction frequencies and recent purchases[cite: 3].
* **High-Value Unhappy (47 Customers)**: High-value spenders dealing with extreme friction from order returns or support issues[cite: 3].

---

## 2. Retention Actions & Capital Prioritization
We optimize our marketing budget by prioritizing cohorts based on their revenue risk and lifetime value:

| Priority | Targeted Segment | Count | Tailored Action Plan | Budget Split |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Priority 1** | At-Risk High-Value | 428 | High-incentive personalized re-engagement offers. | **55%** |
| **Priority 2** | High-Value Unhappy | 47 | Route to specialized support agents to resolve open tickets. | **25%** |
| **Priority 3** | New Shoppers | 515 | Informative content series to build ongoing shopping habits. | **15%** |
| **Priority 4** | Champions | 292 | Non-monetary rewards like early product access and VIP previews. | **5%** |

### Strategic Justification
* We invest **55% of our capital into the At-Risk High-Value cohort** because winning back these high-spending customers delivers the highest immediate return on marketing spend.
* We pause marketing promotions for the **High-Value Unhappy cohort**. Instead, we redirect budget to customer support to resolve outstanding issues, as fixing customer friction builds longer-term retention than price drops[cite: 1, 4].
